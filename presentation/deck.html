The Problem

Every conference we attend, we meet great people — and then lose them

  • Business cards and notes disappear. Nothing lands in a structured system.
  • When a new role opens, recruiters start from zero — even for people they've already met.
  • Not everyone at a conference is actually relevant to it. A DevOps conference draws platform engineers and recruiters, vendors, IT managers — attendance alone proves nothing.
The Solution

An always-on talent pool that tells you who's worth contacting — and why

Every conference contact becomes a searchable, scored, explainable candidate — automatically re-surfaced the moment a matching role opens.

How It Works

Transparent scoring — not a black box

  • Skills 22% · Experience 23% · Title 15%
  • Industry 10% · Mutual Connections 10% · Conference Relevance 10%
  • Every weight is editable in Settings — no hardcoded logic
A domain-relevance gate dampens the score when a title reads as an adjacent, non-technical role — even if they attended the right conference. This is the signal-vs-noise mechanism.
AI's Role

AI explains. It never decides the score.

The deterministic engine is the single source of truth. AI narrates the result in plain language — and every AI output carries its own confidence score, so no claim is ever stated with more certainty than the data supports.

Runs fully on a deterministic fallback with zero LLM cost — and upgrades automatically to a real model the moment an API key is set. Nothing about the scoring changes either way.
