Take-Home Assignment

AI Solution Manager — Recruitment Task

Design and build a system that captures conference attendees as talent leads, enriches them with LinkedIn data, and surfaces the right candidates when a role opens up.

Role: AI Solution Manager Expected effort: 1–2 working days Time to submit: 4–5 days from receipt Language: English

1 Background

TalentOps is the recruiting team at a fast-growing sports-tech company. The team attends multiple industry conferences each year to source top engineering and product talent. These events are among their best channels — the people in the room are often exactly the kind of candidates they're looking for.

The problem: today, conference contacts are not captured anywhere. Recruiters exchange business cards, jot notes on their phones, or scan badges — but nothing lands in a structured system. Within days of the conference, most of these contacts are forgotten. There is no talent pool, no follow-up process, and no way to revisit past event attendees when a new role opens up.

Beyond the capture problem, there's a signal-to-noise challenge: conference attendees are a mixed crowd even within the conference's own topic. A DevOps conference attracts platform engineers and SREs — but also IT managers, network admins, and vendor sales reps. A data engineering summit draws data engineers and ML practitioners — but also business analysts and consultants who attended for adjacent reasons. Any solution needs to automatically distinguish attendees whose actual role matches the conference domain from those who are just noise — without requiring recruiters to screen everyone manually.

The company uses HubSpot as a marketing tool — managing campaigns, contacts, and lead flows — and Comeet as its ATS for active candidates. The recruiting team also has access to LinkedIn. The vision is to extend HubSpot's existing contact infrastructure into a talent pool — a living bank of passive candidates organized by domain, enriched with LinkedIn data, and queryable whenever a new role opens up.

Your mission Design and build a system that turns raw conference attendance into a queryable talent pool — so that when a role opens up, the right candidates surface automatically, with the context a recruiter needs to act.
Note on the data files Because this is a take-home task, the CSV files provided simulate the end state of what a real system would produce — conference attendees as if they had already been captured and exported, LinkedIn data as if it had been pulled via API. Part of your design document should explain how you would build the capture and enrichment layer in a real deployment.

3 What to Build

Build an automated pipeline (script, notebook, or small app — your choice of language/framework) that uses the provided data files to help a recruiter identify the right candidates for a selected open role.

As part of your submission, we expect you to:

  • Define the schema and flow — before building, map out what your pipeline does at each step: what goes in, what happens, and what comes out. This can be a written description, a diagram, or a flow chart — whatever communicates your thinking clearly.
  • Define the output — decide what the output looks like and what fields it contains. The output should be structured and usable by a recruiter without additional processing.
  • Build it — implement the pipeline end-to-end, runnable against the provided CSV files with a selected job_id as input.
Bonus (optional but impressive) Add a simple HTML or CLI summary view that presents the results in a way a recruiter could actually use — without opening a spreadsheet.

5 Assumptions to Address

Before writing a line of code, document your assumptions. A strong submission will state these clearly upfront. Below are the key questions you should answer (either by making an assumption or by asking):

You don't need to implement a solution for every item below — but be ready to share your thinking on how you'd approach them.

  1. How do you define "domain relevance"? A DevOps conference has a clear domain — but how do you determine whether a specific attendee is genuinely a DevOps/platform/SRE professional vs. an IT manager or a vendor rep who happened to attend?
  2. How do you handle a contact with no LinkedIn profile match in the data? Do they get filtered out, or kept with a lower relevance indication?
  3. Is a candidate with 1 mutual connection treated the same as one with 3? Or does the number of connections matter?
  4. Should candidates already in the ATS (Comeet) be flagged differently — e.g., previously rejected candidates?
  5. What is the intended refresh cadence? Is this a one-time batch job or something that runs after every conference?
  6. In a real deployment, who triggers the pipeline — a recruiter manually, or an automated trigger (e.g., after a conference badge scan export)?
  7. Are there privacy/GDPR considerations that would affect how you store or process LinkedIn data at scale?
